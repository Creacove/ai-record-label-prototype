# Mission Pattern Contract

Purpose: define the management capability system behind dynamic mission generation. This document must not box the product into a small starter checklist. The goal is to support elite artist management: the kind of thinking required to take an unknown artist, build leverage, protect the artist, create demand, coordinate specialists, and scale a career across markets over time.

## Core Principle

Mission patterns are not product features. They are adaptable management playbooks.

The Manager should not ask, "Which fixed template do I use?" It should ask:

- What is the artist trying to become?
- What is the current constraint, risk, or opportunity?
- Which management domains are involved?
- What evidence is strong, weak, missing, or contradictory?
- What work should be organized internally?
- What needs human permission before execution?
- What would change the recommendation?

The listed patterns below are not exhaustive. If an artist's situation does not fit an existing pattern, the Manager creates an ad hoc mission pattern and records why.

## Pattern Contract

Each mission pattern must define:

- when to use it
- management domain or domains
- likely agents
- artist-specific inputs required
- evidence needs
- common task types
- checkpoint questions
- permission boundaries
- review triggers
- success state
- blockage/failure state
- what would change the recommendation

Patterns may be composed. A global breakout mission, for example, may combine positioning, market expansion, release strategy, creator/content validation, partnership, touring validation, team operations, and budget allocation.

## Management Domains

These domains describe the range of work the AI label must be prepared to reason about. They are not navigation items and not fixed mission types.

### Career Architecture

Long-term direction, career stage, leverage, sequencing, artist values, creative autonomy, and what the artist should not do.  
Typical questions: What are we building? What should we say no to? What is the next career-level unlock?

### Artist Positioning And Narrative

Story, lane, cultural context, audience thesis, brand posture, visual world, interview angle, and public language.  
Typical questions: Why should people care now? What story travels across markets without flattening the artist?

### A&R And Creative Development

Song selection, focus asset, catalog strategy, collaborations, sonic lane, creative references, and release readiness.  
Typical questions: Which song should lead? What collaboration actually expands the world? Is the material strong enough for the goal?

### Release And Catalog Strategy

Release timing, sequencing, distributor readiness, DSP pitch, content runway, catalog refresh, and post-release reviews.  
Typical questions: Should this release move, wait, split, or become part of a larger campaign?

### Audience And Fan Development

Audience segments, superfans, direct fan capture, community, comments, repeat behavior, creator participation, and demand quality.  
Typical questions: Is attention becoming fandom? Which audience should we deepen before scaling?

### Market Expansion

City, country, diaspora, language, scene, platform, and cultural bridge strategy.  
Typical questions: How does an artist break from local to regional to global? Which market is real enough to test next?

### Marketing And Campaign Operations

Organic/paid campaigns, content systems, creator seeding, press, owned channels, campaign calendars, and iteration.  
Typical questions: What should the team push, test, pause, or change this week?

### Touring And Live Development

Live history, city demand, routing, venues, promoters, support slots, festivals, show readiness, and cost/risk.  
Typical questions: Where can this artist prove live demand without overextending?

### Partnerships, Brand, Sync, And Deals

Brand fit, sync readiness, pitch materials, rights clarity, deal leverage, audience proof, and reputation risk.  
Typical questions: Is this opportunity aligned, credible, and safe?

### Rights, Finance, And Business Affairs

Splits, ownership, publishing, royalty statements, metadata, payout timing, budgets, contracts, and risk controls.  
Typical questions: What can we safely claim, approve, submit, or spend?

### Team, Operations, And Capacity

Roles, deadlines, recurring workflows, approvals, bandwidth, delegation, stakeholder coordination, and process blockers.  
Typical questions: Who owns this, what can realistically happen, and what should be removed?

### Reputation, Crisis, And Wellbeing

Public risk, sensitive decisions, artist wellbeing, burnout, conflict, legal/reputational exposure, and when to slow down.  
Typical questions: What protects the artist and the long-term career even if a short-term metric says push?

### Data Sovereignty And Intelligence

Source connections, uploads, evidence quality, confidence, missing proof, operating memory, and decision auditability.  
Typical questions: What do we need to know before making a stronger call?

## Starter Patterns

These are starter patterns under the management domains. They are examples for generation and testing, not a ceiling.

### Global Breakout Architecture

Use when the artist needs a multi-market path from local momentum to wider cultural and commercial reach.
Domains: Career Architecture, Market Expansion, Artist Positioning, Audience Development, Marketing, Team Operations.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Touring, Sync & Deals, Finance/Rights as needed.
Artist-specific inputs: home market, genre, language/culture context, existing audience pockets, current goal, budget, team capacity, do-not-do rules.
Evidence: streaming geography, social geography, comments, press/context signals, creator participation, direct fan data, prior campaign results.
Tasks: define breakout thesis, identify priority markets, map audience bridges, prepare local-to-global narrative, assign team owners, approve test budget.
Checkpoints: market thesis credible, audience bridge real, team can execute, spend is scoped, next market test is justified.
Permissions: spend, external outreach, public positioning, partnerships.
Success: a sequenced market expansion plan with one or two concrete tests and clear review rules.
Blockage: weak market proof, generic positioning, no team capacity, unclear budget, cultural mismatch.
Change condition: stronger signal emerges in a different market or current market fails review.

### Career Architecture / North Star

Use when the artist's long-term direction, leverage, positioning, or sequence of moves is unclear.
Domains: Career Architecture, Artist Positioning, Team Operations.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Sync & Deals as needed.
Evidence: artist direction, catalog, memory, audience thesis, values, current goals, constraints, prior decisions.
Tasks: define north star, list do-not-do moves, select next career unlock, archive distracting work.
Checkpoints: direction is specific enough to guide decisions, next move supports leverage, risks are named.
Permissions: public positioning or sensitive strategy changes.
Success: reusable career thesis that shapes future missions.
Blockage: conflicting goals, unclear values, team disagreement, generic aspiration.
Change condition: artist changes goal, new market proof appears, or current thesis fails mission reviews.

### A&R / Focus Asset Selection

Use when choosing the song, creative asset, collaboration, or catalog focus that should lead a cycle.
Domains: A&R, Release Strategy, Audience Development.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Sync & Deals as needed.
Evidence: catalog metadata, demos, audience comments, social response, save/listener data if available, creative references.
Tasks: compare focus assets, gather audience proof, define creative rationale, approve focus asset.
Checkpoints: asset matches goal, audience proof is credible, campaign angle is strong enough.
Permissions: public release decision, spend, external pitching.
Success: one clear focus asset with evidence and creative rationale.
Blockage: split attention, weak material, no proof, conflicting artist/team preference.
Change condition: another asset materially outperforms or strategic goal changes.

### Release Planning

Use when preparing, moving, validating, or reviewing a release.
Domains: Release Strategy, Rights/Finance, Marketing, A&R.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Finance/Rights, Sync & Deals as needed.
Evidence: rights/splits, distributor status, DSP pitch readiness, content assets, social signals, budget.
Tasks: confirm rights, submit distributor package, prepare pitch/assets, approve content, verify live links.
Checkpoints: release safety, campaign readiness, launch verification, post-release signal.
Permissions: public date changes, spend, submissions, outreach.
Success: release is safe, promoted appropriately, and reviewed after signal.
Blockage: rights, delivery, missing assets, weak evidence, no approval.
Change condition: rights fail, evidence strengthens/weakens, source data changes, or team capacity changes.

### Budget Allocation

Use when deciding how much to spend, hold, test, or reject.
Domains: Finance/Rights, Marketing, Career Architecture.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Finance/Rights.
Evidence: budget, campaign data, conversion proxies, mission state, risk.
Tasks: approve cap, upload spend history, define test, review results.
Checkpoints: evidence sufficient to spend, holdback preserved, review timing.
Permissions: any spend.
Success: spend decision is scoped, evidenced, and reviewable.
Blockage: weak signal, missing finance data, unclear goal.
Change condition: new conversion proof, budget change, or mission priority change.

### Data / Source Completeness

Use when missing sources block decision quality.
Domains: Data Sovereignty, all relevant domains.
Agents: Manager, all relevant specialists.
Evidence: source readiness, uploads, connector status, missing proof.
Tasks: connect source, upload CSV/file, verify identity, label limitation.
Checkpoints: required sources available, evidence quality sufficient.
Permissions: connector authorization and file upload.
Success: decision confidence improves.
Blockage: source unavailable, bad upload, stale data.
Change condition: source becomes available or mission can proceed with explicit limitation.

### Rights Cleanup

Use when splits, ownership, metadata, or legal/finance confidence blocks action.
Domains: Rights/Finance, Release Strategy, Sync/Deals.
Agents: Finance/Rights, Sync & Deals, Manager.
Evidence: split sheets, metadata, ownership notes, distributor records, statements.
Tasks: upload split sheet, confirm ownership notes, fix metadata, request legal review.
Checkpoints: rights clear enough to proceed, missing proof resolved.
Permissions: legal/finance conclusions and external submissions.
Success: risk reduced and next action safely unlocked.
Blockage: missing signatures, conflicting documents, legal uncertainty.
Change condition: new document appears, conflict resolved, or legal review changes risk.

### Creator / Content Validation

Use when testing content angles, creator fit, or audience participation.
Domains: Marketing, Audience Development, Artist Positioning.
Agents: Marketing, Manager.
Evidence: TikTok/Instagram/YouTube signals, comments, creator list, smart-link data.
Tasks: build creator list, approve drafts, post/test content, upload results.
Checkpoints: attention is repeatable, participation appears, conversion proof exists.
Permissions: spend, outreach, public posts.
Success: clear content angle and next test/scale decision.
Blockage: noisy attention, missing conversion, weak fit.
Change condition: content angle changes, creator niche outperforms, or conversion remains absent.

### City / Live-Market Validation

Use when evaluating city demand, shows, routing, or live opportunity.
Domains: Touring, Market Expansion, Audience Development.
Agents: Touring, Manager, Marketing.
Evidence: city streaming, comments, live history, ticketing proxies, venue notes.
Tasks: upload live history, verify city demand, build venue/promoter list.
Checkpoints: city demand strong enough, show risk acceptable.
Permissions: booking outreach, deposits, public announcements.
Success: city test or routing decision is justified.
Blockage: weak geography, no live history, high cost/risk.
Change condition: stronger city signal appears, live cost changes, or team capacity changes.

### Revenue Investigation

Use when revenue, payout, royalty, or finance questions arise.
Domains: Rights/Finance, Data Sovereignty.
Agents: Finance/Rights, Manager.
Evidence: royalty statements, payout history, distributor reports, campaign spend.
Tasks: upload statements, reconcile period, identify missing source.
Checkpoints: enough evidence to explain revenue movement.
Permissions: financial conclusions and external accounting/legal action.
Success: directional or evidence-backed revenue explanation.
Blockage: missing statements, mismatched periods, incomplete ownership.
Change condition: new statement, corrected period, or ownership update.

### Sync / Deal Readiness

Use when evaluating sync, brand, partnership, or deal opportunity.
Domains: Partnerships/Sync/Deals, Rights/Finance, Artist Positioning.
Agents: Sync & Deals, Finance/Rights, Manager.
Evidence: rights clarity, clean assets, pitch materials, audience proof, brand fit.
Tasks: upload clean assets, build pitch package, confirm rights.
Checkpoints: pitch-ready, rights-safe, fit credible.
Permissions: external pitch/send, deal negotiation.
Success: safe referral or pitch package.
Blockage: rights unclear, missing assets, weak fit.
Change condition: rights clear, assets improve, or opportunity no longer fits.

### Campaign Review

Use when reviewing active or completed campaign performance.
Domains: Marketing, Audience Development, Finance/Rights.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Finance/Rights as needed.
Evidence: campaign spend, content results, smart-link data, platform data, tasks.
Tasks: upload campaign report, compare angles, approve next iteration.
Checkpoints: continue, change, pause, or scale.
Permissions: new spend or external changes.
Success: clear next campaign decision.
Blockage: missing conversion or spend data.
Change condition: new performance report, budget change, or mission priority shift.

### Artist Positioning

Use when shaping artist story, lane, audience thesis, brand posture, or do-not-do rules.
Domains: Artist Positioning, Career Architecture, Marketing.
Agents: Manager, Marketing, Sync & Deals as needed.
Evidence: artist direction, audience comments, catalog, memory, market.
Tasks: review positioning, approve language, reject off-brand moves.
Checkpoints: positioning is clear enough to guide work.
Permissions: public copy or brand commitments.
Success: reusable artist-specific direction.
Blockage: unclear values, conflicting goals, weak audience thesis.
Change condition: audience response, artist preference, or cultural context changes.

### Team Operations

Use when coordinating owners, capacity, recurring work, approvals, or process blockers.
Domains: Team Operations, Career Architecture.
Agents: Manager.
Evidence: tasks, deadlines, user replies, team capacity memory.
Tasks: assign owner, clarify capacity, approve workflow, archive stale work.
Checkpoints: operating process is clear enough to continue.
Permissions: sensitive role/process changes when needed.
Success: fewer blockers and clearer ownership.
Blockage: missing owner, overload, unclear approval chain.
Change condition: team capacity changes or priorities shift.

### Reputation / Crisis / Wellbeing

Use when public risk, burnout, conflict, sensitive decisions, or reputational exposure may harm the artist.
Domains: Reputation, Crisis, Wellbeing, Career Architecture.
Agents: Manager, legal/finance-aware specialists where relevant.
Evidence: user context, public conversation, stakeholder notes, deadlines, workload, risk history.
Tasks: pause risky action, prepare response draft, request human/legal review, reduce workload, set review.
Checkpoints: artist protected, public risk understood, response approved, next step safe.
Permissions: all external/public/sensitive action.
Success: risk contained without damaging long-term leverage.
Blockage: incomplete facts, legal sensitivity, emotional/health risk, public uncertainty.
Change condition: new facts, user approval, legal advice, or public context changes.

## Composition Rules

- One mission can use multiple domains.
- One mission should still have one clear objective.
- If a mission grows into unrelated objectives, Manager synthesis should split it into separate missions.
- If a pattern starts to constrain the right answer, the Manager creates an ad hoc pattern.
- Agent reports can propose patterns, but Manager synthesis selects or adapts them.

## Acceptance Checks

- The system can create a non-release mission without borrowing release tasks/checkpoints.
- A global breakout mission combines multiple domains without becoming vague.
- Every pattern produces artist-specific tasks and checkpoints.
- Every pattern states evidence, permission boundaries, and change conditions.
- The Manager can say "this does not fit an existing pattern" and create an ad hoc pattern.
